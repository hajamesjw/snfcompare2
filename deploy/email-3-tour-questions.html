Your Tour Checklist
8 Questions to Ask — and How to Verify the Answers
Question 1
“What is your current nurse-to-resident ratio on each shift?”
Why it matters: Staffing ratios determine how quickly your loved one gets help when they press the call button. Daytime ratios look different from night shift — always ask about both.
Cross-reference: Check the Staffing rating and total nursing hours per resident per day on SNF Compare.
Question 2
“What were the findings from your most recent state inspection?”
Why it matters: Their answer will reveal how transparent they are. Facilities that are proud of their record will discuss it openly. Those that deflect or minimize should raise your guard.
Cross-reference: View the Health Inspection rating, deficiency count, and severity on SNF Compare.
Question 3
“How many complaints have been filed against this facility in the past three years?”
Why it matters: Substantiated complaints are verified by government investigators. They indicate real, confirmed problems — not just grievances. A facility that claims zero complaints may not be telling the full story.
Cross-reference: Check for substantiated complaints and complaint survey deficiencies on SNF Compare.
Question 4
“Has this facility been fined or penalized by CMS in the past three years?”
Why it matters: Fines mean CMS found violations serious enough to warrant financial punishment. Payment denials are even more severe. Ask directly — and watch how they respond.
Cross-reference: View the full penalty history including fine amounts and dates on SNF Compare.
Question 5
“What is your staff turnover rate for nursing staff?”
Why it matters: High turnover means your loved one will constantly be cared for by unfamiliar staff who don’t know their preferences, medical history, or routines. Continuity of care is critical for vulnerable residents.
Cross-reference: Check the RN and total nursing staff turnover percentages on SNF Compare.
Question 6
“What percentage of your residents have been hospitalized in the past year?”
Why it matters: High rehospitalization rates can signal inadequate monitoring, poor medication management, or slow response to health changes. A well-run facility catches problems early and avoids preventable hospital transfers.
Cross-reference: Review the Quality Measures rating and rehospitalization metrics on SNF Compare.
Question 7
“What is your policy on antipsychotic medication use?”
Why it matters: Antipsychotic medications are sometimes used as chemical restraints to manage resident behavior rather than addressing the underlying cause. CMS tracks this as a key quality indicator. Lower usage rates generally indicate better person-centered care.
Cross-reference: Check the antipsychotic medication usage percentage under Quality Measures on SNF Compare.
Question 8
“Has the ownership of this facility changed in the past five years?”
Why it matters: Ownership changes often bring new management, budget restructuring, and staffing changes. A facility that was excellent under previous ownership may be very different today. Recent ownership changes are especially worth scrutinizing.
Cross-reference: View ownership information and rating trends over time on SNF Compare.
