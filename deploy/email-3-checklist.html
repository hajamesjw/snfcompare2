The 7-Point Facility Checklist
✓
Look up the facility's overall star rating. Start with the big picture. CMS rates every facility on a 1-to-5 scale. While no single rating tells the whole story, anything below 3 stars deserves extra scrutiny. SNF Compare surfaces this rating instantly for every facility.
✓
Check the estimated hourly wage for your role. Filter by NP, RN, LPN, or CNA to see estimated compensation. Compare it to other facilities in the same state. If their offer is significantly below the area average, you have leverage — or a clear signal to keep looking.
✓
Review staff turnover rates. This is the canary in the coal mine. High turnover means chronic dissatisfaction. Sort by turnover to see where any facility ranks against its peers. Target facilities below 35% for the best working conditions.
✓
Examine staffing hours per resident per day. Click into the facility detail page to see actual reported staffing levels. Are RN hours adequate? How about total nursing hours? Facilities below the CMS-recommended 3.48 total nursing hours are understaffed — and that means your shifts will feel it.
✓
Read the inspection deficiency history. Look at both the number and severity of deficiencies. A few minor citations are normal. A pattern of serious or repeated deficiencies signals systemic problems that won't be fixed by one good nurse. Pay special attention to "immediate jeopardy" findings.
✓
Check for fines and payment denials. Financial penalties indicate the most severe compliance failures. If a facility has been fined or had Medicare payments denied, that's CMS saying the problems are serious enough to warrant financial consequences. This information is available on the SNF Compare facility detail page.
✓
Compare against 3-5 nearby alternatives. Never evaluate a facility in isolation. Use SNF Compare to pull up neighboring facilities in the same state and compare them side by side. Knowing what "normal" looks like in your area gives you the context you need to spot outliers — both good and bad.
 
What veterans do differently
Experienced nurses don't just look at one data point — they look for consistency. A facility with moderate pay but exceptional turnover and staffing numbers is often a far better workplace than one with high pay but revolving-door staff.
The best strategy: identify the top 3 facilities in your area across all metrics, not just one. Those are the places where nurses stay — and where you'll want to be.
