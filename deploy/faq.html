Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about choosing a nursing home and using SNF Compare.

About Nursing Homes

What is a skilled nursing facility (SNF)?

A skilled nursing facility (SNF) provides 24-hour nursing care and rehabilitation services for people who need medical care but don't require hospitalization. SNFs offer:

  • Physical, occupational, and speech therapy
  • Wound care and IV medications
  • Post-surgical recovery care
  • Long-term care for chronic conditions
  • Memory care for dementia patients

What's the difference between a nursing home and assisted living?

Nursing homes (SNFs) provide skilled medical care with licensed nurses on duty 24/7. They're designed for people who need regular medical attention, rehabilitation, or help with most daily activities.

Assisted living provides help with daily activities (bathing, dressing, meals) but less intensive medical care. Residents are generally more independent and don't require constant nursing supervision.

How long do people typically stay in a nursing home?

It varies significantly based on the reason for admission:

  • Short-stay rehabilitation: 2-4 weeks (post-surgery, stroke recovery)
  • Post-acute care: 1-3 months (recovering from serious illness)
  • Long-term care: Months to years (chronic conditions, dementia)

About 25% of residents stay less than 3 months, while others may reside for years.

Understanding Ratings

How are nursing homes rated?

CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) rates nursing homes on a 1 to 5 star scale based on three components:

  • Health Inspections (most weighted): Results from state surveys over the past 3 years
  • Staffing: Hours of care provided per resident per day
  • Quality Measures: Clinical outcomes like falls, infections, and readmissions

The overall rating combines these factors, with health inspections having the most impact.

Can I trust the star ratings?

Star ratings are a useful starting point but have limitations:

  • Quality measures are self-reported by facilities
  • Inspections are snapshots in time
  • A facility might excel in one area but struggle in another

We recommend using ratings to narrow your options, then visiting facilities in person and speaking with current residents and families.

What is "Hours Per Resident Day" (HPRD)?

HPRD measures how much nursing time each resident receives on average per day. For example, if a facility has 4.0 total nursing HPRD, each resident receives an average of 4 hours of direct nursing care daily.

Higher HPRD generally correlates with better outcomes. Research suggests 4.1+ total HPRD is ideal, with at least 0.75 hours from registered nurses (RNs).

Costs & Coverage

Does Medicare cover nursing home care?

Medicare covers skilled nursing care (not custodial care) under specific conditions:

  • You had a qualifying hospital stay of at least 3 days
  • You enter a Medicare-certified SNF within 30 days of discharge
  • You need skilled nursing or therapy services

Coverage: Days 1-20: $0 copay | Days 21-100: ~$200/day copay (2024) | After 100 days: Not covered

Does Medicaid cover nursing home care?

Yes, Medicaid is the largest payer for nursing home care, covering about 60% of all nursing home residents. Unlike Medicare, Medicaid covers long-term custodial care.

To qualify, you must meet income and asset limits, which vary by state. Many people "spend down" their assets to become eligible. An elder law attorney can help with Medicaid planning.

How much does nursing home care cost?

Costs vary significantly by location and level of care:

  • National median (2024): ~$300/day for a semi-private room
  • Private room: ~$340/day or ~$125,000/year
  • Range: $200-$500+/day depending on location and services

Urban areas and states like California, New York, and Massachusetts tend to have higher costs.

Using SNF Compare

Where does SNF Compare get its data?

Our data comes from official CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) databases, including:

  • Nursing Home Compare data (star ratings, inspection results)
  • Payroll-Based Journal staffing data
  • Quality measures from MDS assessments
  • Provider enrollment data

We update our database regularly as CMS releases new information.

How often is the data updated?

CMS updates nursing home data monthly. We refresh our database within 1-2 weeks of each CMS release. However, some data (like inspection results) may reflect conditions from several months ago, as inspections happen on an annual cycle.

Is SNF Compare free to use?

Yes, SNF Compare is completely free. We believe everyone should have access to quality information when making healthcare decisions for themselves or their loved ones.
