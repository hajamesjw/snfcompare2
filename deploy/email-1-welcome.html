The 4 CMS Rating Categories You Need to Understand
CMS rates every nursing home on a 1–5 star scale across four categories. Here’s what each one actually measures — and why it matters for your family.
★
Overall Rating
The composite score CMS assigns based on all three categories below. This is the headline number — but it can mask serious problems in individual areas. Always look at the breakdown.
🔎
Health Inspection Rating
Based on unannounced on-site inspections over the past 3 years. This is the most important rating because it reflects what government inspectors actually observed — not what the facility self-reports. Deficiencies, severity, and patterns are all factored in.
♥
Quality Measures Rating
Tracks clinical outcomes: falls with injury, pressure ulcers, UTIs, use of antipsychotic medications, rehospitalizations, and more. These metrics reveal how well residents are actually being cared for on a day-to-day basis. Higher stars mean better outcomes.
👥
Staffing Rating
Measures the number of hours of care provided per resident per day by RNs, LPNs, CNAs, and physical therapists. Research consistently shows that staffing levels are the single strongest predictor of care quality. Low staffing means longer response times, missed medications, and higher fall risk.
⚠ A word of caution
Never rely on a facility’s own marketing materials, website testimonials, or tour impressions alone. These are curated presentations. CMS inspection data is collected by independent government surveyors during unannounced visits — it’s the closest thing to an unfiltered look at how a facility actually operates. Always verify the data.
