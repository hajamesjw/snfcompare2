How to Use SNF Compare

A comprehensive guide to understanding nursing home ratings and finding the right facility for your loved one.

1 Understanding the Star Ratings

CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) rates nursing homes on a 1 to 5 star scale, with 5 being the best. The overall rating combines three separate components:

★★★★★

5 Stars - Much Above Average

Top performers in quality, staffing, and inspections.

★★★★☆

4 Stars - Above Average

Better than most facilities in your area.

★★★☆☆

3 Stars - Average

Meets expectations but room for improvement.

★★☆☆☆

2 Stars - Below Average

Some concerns; investigate further before choosing.

★☆☆☆☆

1 Star - Much Below Average

Significant quality concerns identified.

Pro Tip

Don't rely solely on the overall rating. A facility might have 5 stars for quality measures but lower staffing scores. Look at each component individually.

2 The Three Rating Components

Health Inspections Rating

Based on the last 3 years of state health inspections. Surveyors visit unannounced and check for compliance with federal quality standards. More recent inspections carry more weight.

Staffing Rating

Measures the amount of care provided by nurses and aides. Higher staffing levels generally lead to better resident outcomes. This is measured in Hours Per Resident Day (HPRD).

Quality Measures Rating

Based on clinical outcomes like hospital readmissions, falls, pressure ulcers, and medication management. These metrics come from the facility's own reported data.

3 Understanding Staffing Metrics

Staffing is one of the most important factors in quality care. Here's what the numbers mean:

Metric What It Measures Good Target
Total Nursing HPRD Total hours of nursing care per resident per day 4.0+ hours
RN HPRD Registered nurse hours per resident per day 0.75+ hours
CNA HPRD Certified nursing assistant hours per resident per day 2.5+ hours
Staff Turnover Percentage of staff who left in past year Under 50%

Why Staffing Matters

Research shows facilities with higher staffing levels have fewer falls, pressure ulcers, infections, and hospital readmissions. Staff turnover affects continuity of care.

4 Using the Compare Tool

Our comparison tool lets you evaluate up to 3 facilities side-by-side. Here's how to get the most out of it:

Step 1: Search for Facilities

Enter a city, state, or facility name in the search box. Results update as you type.

Step 2: Choose Your View

Family View: Simplified metrics focused on what matters most to families choosing care.
Professional View: Detailed data including wages, turnover rates, and technical metrics.

Step 3: Select Facilities to Compare

Click the checkbox on up to 3 facility cards. Your selections appear in the tray at the bottom.

Step 4: Review and Decide

Click "Compare Selected" to see a detailed side-by-side comparison of your chosen facilities.

5 Questions to Ask During Tours

Once you've narrowed down your choices, visit each facility and ask:

  • What is your current staffing ratio?
  • Can I see a copy of your most recent inspection report?
  • How do you handle medical emergencies?
  • What activities and therapies do you offer?
  • How do you communicate with families?
  • What are the total costs, including any additional fees?
  • Can I speak with current residents or family members?
